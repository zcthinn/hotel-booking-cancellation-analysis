Key finding: Deposit policy is the most powerful single lever for reducing cancellations. Bookings with a refundable deposit cancel at 99.4% — essentially a placeholder with no commitment. No-deposit bookings (87.9% of volume) cancel at 40.8%. Non-refundable bookings, while only 11.5% of volume, cancel at just 22% — confirming that financial commitment dramatically increases guest follow-through. Expanding non-refundable rates, especially for high-risk lead-time segments, is the highest-ROI policy change available.

Management Recommendations

Data-backed actions to reduce cancellation rate and protect revenue
Immediate · Deposit Policy
−18%
Tiered Non-Refundable Deposit by Lead Time
Mandate 20–40% non-refundable deposit for bookings >90 days out, scaling to 50% for >365 days. Modelled to reduce cancellation rate by ~18% in high-risk segments and recover €2.0–€2.8M annually.
Short-term · Channel Mix
+€1.5M
Incentivise Direct Bookings Over OTA
OTA-sourced bookings carry 15–20% higher cancellation rates than direct. Loyalty discounts, exclusive room tiers, and upgrade offers for direct bookers could shift ~10% of OTA volume, recovering an estimated €1.5M.
Operational · City Hotel
41.8%
City Hotel Requires Dedicated Intervention
At 41.8% vs Resort's 27.0%, City Hotel accounts for 76% of all cancellations. A targeted overbooking buffer of 8–12% during Q2–Q3, combined with waitlist management, would minimise revenue gaps on high-demand nights.
Strategic · Pricing
Jul–Aug
Dynamic Pricing Calibrated to Cancel Risk
July–August show highest ADR (€126–€140) alongside elevated cancellation volumes. Implement dynamic rates that price in cancellation probability — raising base rates for flexible bookings and discounting locked-in non-refundable rates.
Revenue Recovery · Upsell
+€0.9M
Cancellation-to-Waitlist Conversion Programme
Automate same-day cancellation alerts to a pre-qualified waitlist. Historical data shows 2,400+ same-day cancellations annually — converting 30–35% to same-night bookings at peak-season ADR yields ~€0.9M incremental revenue.
Long-term · Forecasting
+169%
Build Cancellation Prediction Model
Revenue grew 169% from 2015 to 2016, with continued ADR growth into 2017. A predictive ML model using lead time, channel, country, meal type, and season could flag bookings with >60% cancel probability at intake for proactive intervention.
